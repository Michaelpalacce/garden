# Core Principles

- **Clarity** is paramount. Use precise and unambiguous language.
	- Provide a glossary for technical terms
	- Provide acronyms and their meaning
	- Each sentence should convey a single, clear idea.
- **Conciseness** is important. Be **brief and to the point**.
	- Remove unnecessary words, phrases and redundancy ("filler").
	- Shorter sentences improve readability and [[Scannability\|scannability]] of text.
- **Accuracy** of the information is important. The Architect needs to be aware of the deep technical details of what he is overseeing. 
- Track **Audience Awareness** and change your approach of needed. Tailor the content accordingly based on technical background and understanding
- **Structure & Organize** the documentation in clear headings, subheadings and paragraphs. 
- **Completeness** of the information helps remove misunderstandings. The documents provided by architects should give enough information and provide enough context. 
- Be **Consistent** in grammar, tone, style and formatting.
